Question
========

*Based on the $p$-value just obtained, what would be your conclusion about the relationship between maximal heart rate and age?*

*The correlation between the two variables is:*

Answerlist
----------
* *significantly negative with $r<0$*
* *significantly positive with $r>0$*
* *significantly negative with $\rho<-0.70$*
* *not significant, as $P(\rho<-0.70)>\alpha$*
* *not significant, as $P(\rho<-0.70)<\alpha$*

Solution
========

The probability of observing a Fisher-transformed correlation coefficient smaller than that associated with $\rho=-0.70$ is smaller than $\alpha = 0.05$.

For this reason, there is evidence that the population correlation coefficient is smaller than $-0.70$. The correlation between maximal heart rate and age is therefore significantly negative and stronger than the threshold considered physiologically relevant.
