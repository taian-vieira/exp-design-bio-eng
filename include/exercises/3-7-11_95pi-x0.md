Question
========

*Knowing that the 95% prediction interval, within which 95% of predictions for subjects with the same jump height and body mass values would be expected, is computed as:*

$$
\hat{y}_0 \pm t_{1-\frac{\alpha}{2},\nu=n-p-1} s_{\hat{y}_0},
$$

*what would be the extremes defining this interval?*

:::: {.columns}
::: {.column width="50%"}
*The lower extreme is: ##ANSWER1##*
:::
::: {.column width="50%"}
*The upper extreme is: ##ANSWER2##*
:::
::::

Answerlist
----------

Solution
========

Based on the critical $t$ value

$$
t_{0.975,17} = `r round(qt(0.975, 17),3)`
$$

and on the values defined above for the individual prediction $\hat{y}_0$ and its standard error $s_{\hat{y}_0}$, the extremes of the 95% prediction interval are obtained as:

$$
(`r pi95[1]`,`r pi95[2]`)
$$
