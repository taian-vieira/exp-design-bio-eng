##### Exercise: VIF in anaerobic power prediction

*The figure below reports the Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) computed for three explanatory variables in the full model ($X_1$, $X_2$, and $X_3$) and for two explanatory variables in the reduced model ($X_1$ and $X_2$).*

![](/include/images/part_3/ch_7/vif-example.png){width="80%" fig-align="center"}

*What is the most appropriate conclusion?*

Answerlist
----------
* All explanatory variables should be removed from the model.
* There is evidence of severe multicollinearity between body mass ($X_2$) and BMI ($X_3$), suggesting that one of them should be excluded from the model.
* Jump height ($X_1$) should be excluded from the model because its VIF is greater than 1.
* The reduced model should be discarded because the VIF values are smaller than those of the full model.

Solution
========

The full model yields extremely large VIF values for body mass ($X_2$) and BMI ($X_3$), indicating that these variables contain largely redundant information.

The reduced model, obtained after removing BMI, yields VIF values close to 1 for both remaining predictors. Therefore, the most appropriate conclusion is that body mass and BMI exhibit severe multicollinearity and one of them should be excluded from the model.
