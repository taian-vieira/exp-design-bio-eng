Question
========

*Based on the figure above and considering the question---"Which intervention would lead to greater weight loss?"--- what would be the null and the alternative hypotheses?*

Answerlist
----------
* $H_0: \mu_d = 0, \mu_e = 0$, $H_1: \mu_d=\mu_e$
* $H_0: \mu_d = \mu_e$, $H_1: \mu_d \ne \mu_e$
* $H_0: \mu_d = 0, \mu_e = 0$, $H_1: \mu_d \ne \mu_e$
* $H_0: \mu_d = \mu_e$, $H_1: \mu_d < \mu_e$
* *none of the above*


Solution
========

The null hypothesis state that there is no difference between treatments. This would se the basis for defining the distribution of the test statistic under the $H_0$

Given the question posed does not indicate a direction for the expected differences, the alternative hypothesis states a difference between mean without a specific direction.
